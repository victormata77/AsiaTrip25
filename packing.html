Smart Packing Tips

Pack Light, Pack Smart

Remember the "one-week rule" – pack for one week, even for longer trips. Laundry services are available at all our hotels.

Pro Tip: Wear your bulkiest items during flights to save luggage space.

Mix & Match Clothing

Choose a color scheme and pack items that can be mixed and matched to create multiple outfits.

Pro Tip: Aim for a 3:1 ratio – three tops for every bottom.

Roll, Don't Fold

Rolling clothes instead of folding saves space and reduces wrinkles. Use packing cubes to keep everything organized.

Pro Tip: Pack heavier items at the bottom of your suitcase near the wheels.

Tech Essentials

Universal adapter, portable charger, and extra memory cards for photos. Keep chargers in your carry-on.

Pro Tip: Take photos of important documents as backups.

Medications & First Aid

Pack more medication than you think you'll need. Bring prescriptions in original packaging with labels.

Pro Tip: Basic first aid kit with painkillers, band-aids, antiseptic wipes, and rehydration salts.

Carry-On Essentials

Pack a change of clothes, basic toiletries, medications, and entertainment in your carry-on in case of lost luggage.

Pro Tip: Bring an empty water bottle to fill after security.

General Tropical Items

  • High SPF sunscreen (reef-safe preferred)
  • After-sun lotion or aloe vera gel
  • Insect repellent with DEET
  • Electrolyte tablets/powder for hydration
  • Lightweight, quick-dry clothing
class="flex items-start"> Create a detailed list and check items off as you pack
  • Use packing cubes to organize and compress clothing
  • Pack items that serve multiple purposes (e.g., sarong as beach cover, scarf, blanket)
  • Leave room for souvenirs and purchases
  • Pack a foldable tote bag for day trips and shopping
  • Include essential toiletries like sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Place a luggage tag with contact details on each bag

    • Packing Don'ts

    • Don't pack full-sized toiletries – travel sizes save space
    • Don't bring items you "might" use – stick to essentials
    • Don't pack valuables in checked luggage
    • Don't pack too many shoes – limit to 3 versatile pairs
    • Don't fold what can be rolled to save space
    • Don't wait until the last minute to pack
    • Don't forget to check airline baggage restrictions for each flight

    Destination-Specific Items

    South Korea (Seoul)

    • T-Money card for public transportation (purchase upon arrival)
    • Light rain jacket (summer showers)
    • Portable hand towel (many restrooms don't provide paper towels)
    • Type C & F adapters for electronics

    Climate:

    Hot and humid (30-34°C) with occasional showers

    Japan (Tokyo & Kyoto)

    • Suica/PASMO card for public transport (purchase upon arrival)
    • Modest clothing for temple visits (shoulders & knees covered)
    • Portable hand towel/handkerchief (common in Japan)
    • Slip-on shoes (for frequent removal at temples/restaurants)
    • Type A & B adapters for electronics

    Climate:

    Hot and humid (28-33°C) with high humidity and possible showers

    Singapore

    • Light, breathable clothing (air conditioning is very cold indoors)
    • Small umbrella for sudden showers
    • Swimming attire for Villa Hotel Sentosa pools
    • Type G adapters for electronics

    Climate:

    Hot and humid year-round (30-32°C) with brief showers

    Malaysia (Kuala Lumpur)

    • Modest clothing for visiting Batu Caves (shoulders & knees covered)
    • Comfortable shoes for cave steps (272 steps)
    • Type G adapters for electronics

    Climate:

    Hot and humid (30-34°C) with afternoon thunderstorms

    Maldives

    • High SPF, reef-safe sunscreen (essential)
    • Multiple swimsuits (you'll use them daily)
    • Rash guard/UV protection swim shirt
    • Snorkeling equipment (or rent at resort)
    • Waterproof case for smartphone
    • Insect repellent for evenings
    • Light layers for evenings
