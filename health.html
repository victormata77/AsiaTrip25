Health & Safety

Essential information for a safe and worry-free journey

Safety Overview

Our trip will take us through some of the safest destinations in Asia. All countries on our itinerary are considered safe for tourists with normal precautions. Each destination has its own unique considerations, but overall, these are well-developed tourism destinations with good infrastructure and healthcare facilities.

While our trip is planned for July-August 2025, which generally offers favorable weather conditions, it's important to note this is the rainy season in some destinations. The main health considerations will be:

  • Heat and humidity management, particularly in Singapore, Malaysia, and the Maldives
  • Protection against mosquito-borne illnesses
  • Food and water safety practices
  • Sun protection, especially in the Maldives

Each family member has been assigned a safety buddy, and we'll use the WhatsApp group "Mata Asia 2025" for all communications during the trip.

Health Preparation

Prescription Medications

Pack enough for the entire trip plus a 1-week buffer. Carry prescriptions with generic names. Keep medications in original packaging with labels.

First Aid Kit

Our family travel kit includes pain relievers, anti-diarrheal medication, antihistamines, motion sickness remedies, band-aids, antiseptic wipes, and digital thermometer.

Insect-Borne Disease Prevention

Use insect repellent with 20-30% DEET, especially in Malaysia and the Maldives. Wear long sleeves and pants at dawn/dusk when mosquitoes are most active.

Food & Water Safety

Drink bottled or purified water. Be cautious with street food, especially in Malaysia. Avoid raw or undercooked meat, fish, and eggs.

Sun Protection

Use broad-spectrum SPF 50+ sunscreen, especially in the Maldives where UV index is extremely high. Reapply every 2 hours when outdoors.

Pre-Trip Medical Checklist

  • General check-up for all family members (scheduled: June 1, 2025)
  • Dental check-up (scheduled: June 5, 2025)
  • Renew all prescriptions with 3-month supply
  • Prepare medical history summary for each person
  • Check if insurance covers pre-existing conditions

Special Health Considerations

Heat & Humidity

All destinations will be hot and humid in July-August. Plan for frequent hydration and rest breaks. Carry electrolyte packets to prevent dehydration.

Jet Lag Management

East Asia is 7 hours ahead of Spain. Begin adjusting sleep schedules 3 days before departure. Stay hydrated during flights and avoid alcohol.

Water Activities Safety

For Maldives snorkeling, always use buddy system and never swim alone. Be aware of currents and follow resort safety guidelines.

Food Allergies

Mom's shellfish allergy: Carry EpiPen in carry-on luggage. Learn to communicate this allergy in local languages (translation cards prepared).

Teen-Specific Health Notes

  • Ensure teens drink sufficient water (aim for 2-3 liters daily)
  • Protect electronics from heat and humidity damage
  • Review sun safety with everyone, especially for water activities
  • Daily check-ins about any health concerns
  • Remind teens to alert parents about any symptoms promptly

Family Emergency Response Plan

Family Emergency Protocol

In case of separation or emergency, we will follow these steps:

  1. Stay calm and assess the situation
  2. Call or message the family WhatsApp group "Mata Asia 2025"
  3. If separated, return to the most recent hotel or predetermined meeting point
  4. If unable to reach family members, contact the Spanish embassy or consulate
  5. For medical emergencies, first call local emergency number, then notify family
