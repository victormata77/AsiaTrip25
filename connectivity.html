Connectivity Overview

Staying connected during our multi-country journey will be essential for navigation, translation, emergency contacts, and sharing our adventures with friends and family. This guide provides everything you need to know about mobile data, WiFi access, power adapters, and essential travel apps for our trip through South Korea, Japan, Singapore, Malaysia, and the Maldives.

While international roaming is an option, it can be extremely expensive. This guide focuses on cost-effective alternatives that will keep us connected without breaking the bank. Our connectivity strategy includes:

  • Using eSIM technology for data in each country
  • Leveraging hotel WiFi and free public WiFi where available
  • Having the right power adapters for each destination
  • Installing essential travel apps before departure
  • Setting up VPN for secure connections when needed

For families, we've prepared a structured approach with options for individual connectivity and shared data plans, depending on everyone's needs.

eSIM Options

What is an eSIM?

An eSIM (embedded SIM) is a digital SIM that allows you to activate a cellular plan without having to use a physical SIM card. It's the most convenient option for international travel as you can:

Keep your physical SIM

Maintain your Spanish number for calls/texts while using eSIM for data

Switch between countries

Install multiple eSIMs and switch as we travel between countries

Activate instantly

Scan a QR code to install and activate without physical cards

Device Compatibility

Alternative Data Options

Voltage Considerations

Voltage by Country

  • South Korea: 220V, 60Hz
  • Japan: 100V, 50/60Hz
  • Singapore: 230V, 50Hz
  • Malaysia: 240V, 50Hz
  • Maldives: 230V, 50Hz
  • Spain (reference): 230V, 50Hz

Important Note for Japan:

Japan's 100V system is lower than Europe's 230V. Most modern electronics (phones, laptops, cameras) have dual-voltage power supplies (100-240V) and will work fine, but check your devices to be sure.

Hair dryers, straighteners, and other high-power devices may not work properly or could be damaged unless they specifically support 100V.

Recommended Adapters

Recommended: EPICKA Universal Travel Adapter or similar with multiple USB ports and USB-C compatibility.

Our Family Strategy

  • 2 universal adapters for the whole family
  • 2 multi-port USB charging stations
  • Each person brings their own charging cables
  • Power strips are not necessary as our hotels have multiple outlets

Charging Tips for Family Travel

  • Establish a "charging station" in each hotel room where devices are charged
  • Charge phones overnight and portable power banks during the day
  • Label everyone's cables and adapters to avoid mix-ups
  • Always pack charging cables in carry-on, not checked luggage
  • Don't leave chargers plugged in without devices (wastes electricity and outlet space)
  • Don't use non-certified charging cables (safety risk)
  • Don't overload adapters with heavy devices
  • Don't assume hotel rooms will have easily accessible outlets

VPN & Security

What is a VPN and Why Do We Need It?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) creates a secure, encrypted connection between your device and the internet. When traveling abroad, using a VPN is important for several reasons:

Security on Public WiFi

Protects your data when using hotel or public WiFi networks, which can be vulnerable to hackers

Access Spanish Content

Connect to Spanish servers to access banking, streaming services, or websites that might be restricted abroad

Privacy Protection

Prevent your browsing activity from being monitored or tracked by networks, websites, or apps

When to Use VPN During Our Trip

Always Use VPN When:

  • Connecting to public WiFi networks in airports, cafes, or hotels
  • Accessing banking websites or apps
  • Making online purchases or entering payment information
  • Accessing Spanish streaming services (Netflix Spain, etc.)
  • Using social media on unsecured networks

VPN May Not Be Necessary When:

  • Using cellular data through your eSIM (already relatively secure)
  • Connecting to secure, password-protected hotel WiFi for basic browsing
  • Using apps that have their own encryption (WhatsApp, banking apps)
  • When connection speed is critical and security risk is low
  • Note: When in doubt, use the VPN for better security

Family Setup:

NordVPN is installed on all family smartphones and tablets. Parents' devices are configured to connect automatically on unknown WiFi networks. A reminder card with VPN instructions is in our shared Google Drive folder.
