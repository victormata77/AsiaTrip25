Where tradition meets futuristic innovation

Welcome to Tokyo

Tokyo, Japan's bustling capital, is a fascinating blend of ultramodern and traditional. This dynamic metropolis offers an incredible mix of cutting-edge technology, world-class food, historic temples, dazzling neon lights, and tranquil gardens. During our four-night stay, we'll explore both the spiritual heart and futuristic visions of this extraordinary city, experiencing the perfect balance of cultural immersion and family-friendly entertainment.

Essential Information

Weather in August

  • • Average high: 31-33°C (88-91°F)
  • • Average low: 23-25°C (73-77°F)
  • • Humidity: Very high (75-85%)
  • • Rainfall: Frequent, usually brief showers
  • • Daylight: Sunrise ~5:00 AM, Sunset ~6:45 PM

Pack lightweight, breathable clothing, rain gear, and portable fans or cooling towels for the summer heat.

Currency

  • • Japanese Yen (¥/JPY)
  • • €1 ≈ ¥165 (April 2025 rate)
  • • Cash is still king in many places
  • • Credit cards accepted at hotels, department stores
  • • Seven Bank ATMs accept international cards

Tipping

Not expected or practiced in Japan. Service charge may be included at upscale restaurants and hotels.

Getting Around

  • • Suica/PASMO Card: Essential for trains, buses, and convenience stores
  • • JR Lines: Extensive rail network (consider 72-hour Subway Ticket)
  • • Tokyo Metro: Efficient subway system with English signage
  • • Taxis: Reliable but expensive, doors open automatically
  • • Walking: Many attractions can be reached on foot within districts

Our hotel is a 10-minute walk from Tokyo Station, offering excellent connections throughout the city. Hibiya and Ginza lines provide direct access to popular areas.

Must-Try Foods

  • • Sushi: Fresh seafood over vinegared rice (try at Tsukiji)
  • • Ramen: Hearty noodle soup with various broths
  • • Tempura: Lightly battered and fried seafood or vegetables
  • • Yakitori: Grilled chicken skewers
  • • Tonkatsu: Crispy breaded pork cutlets
  • • Matcha: Green tea in various forms (ice cream, cakes)

Dining Etiquette

Say "itadakimasu" before eating, don't stick chopsticks upright in rice, slurping noodles is acceptable and even appreciated, and no tipping.

Language & Communication

Useful Japanese Phrases:

Hello こんにちは (Konnichiwa)
Thank you ありがとう (Arigatou)
Yes/No はい/いいえ (Hai/Iie)
Excuse me すみません (Sumimasen)
Where is... ...はどこですか? (...wa doko desuka?)
How much? いくらですか? (Ikura desuka?)

English signage is common in Tokyo, especially in tourist areas and train stations. Download Google Translate with Japanese language pack for offline use.

Cultural Tips

  • • Bow when greeting people (depth varies by formality)
  • • Remove shoes when entering homes, traditional restaurants, and some attractions
  • • Avoid eating while walking (except in designated food areas)
  • • Keep voices low in public transportation
  • • Carry a small towel for drying hands (many public restrooms don't have paper towels)
  • • Queue in orderly lines for trains, buses, and escalators

Important Note

Japan values politeness, cleanliness, and respect for personal space. Follow locals' lead on public behavior, and you'll fit right in.

Activities & Itinerary

Monday, August 4, 2025 – Arrival

Day 5

Nihombashi Area

Historic district with modern amenities

  • 16:15: Arrival at Narita Airport
  • 17:00-18:00: Transport to hotel via Airport Limousine Bus or Narita Express
  • 18:00: Check-in at MIMARU SUITES Tokyo Nihombashi
  • 19:00-21:00: Evening exploration of Nihombashi area
    • Coredo Muromachi complex (historical shopping and dining)
    • Dinner at Nihonbashi Tamai for authentic Japanese cuisine

Tip: Nihombashi (literally "Japan Bridge") marks the starting point of all roads in Japan and features a beautiful mix of historical buildings and modern department stores. The Coredo Muromachi complex houses specialty shops selling traditional crafts alongside modern Japanese brands.

Tuesday, August 5, 2025 – Classic Tokyo & Gaming

Day 6

Meiji Shrine & Harajuku

Sacred forest and trendy fashion district

  • 08:30-09:30: Breakfast at hotel or local café
  • 10:00-11:30: Meiji Shrine & Yoyogi Park
    • Serene forest walk and traditional Shinto shrine
    • Possible traditional wedding sightings on weekends

    • Takeshita Street (teen fashion hub)

Highlight: Shibuya Crossing is the world's busiest intersection, with up to 3,000 people crossing at once during peak times. The organized chaos as the lights change is a quintessential Tokyo experience and perfect for photos or videos.

Wednesday, August 6, 2025 – Traditional & Modern Tokyo

Day 7

Sensō-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest Buddhist temple

  • 08:30-09:30: Breakfast at hotel
  • 10:00-12:00: Split morning activities:
    • Group 1: Samurai Museum Tokyo (Shinjuku) with armor try-on and sword demonstration
    • Group 2: Anime Workshop in Akihabara with character design and voice acting experience
  • 12:30-13:30: Meet for lunch in Asakusa
  • 14:00-15:30: Sensō-ji Temple & Nakamise Shopping Street

  • 18:00-21:00: Akihabara Electric Town

Highlight: Sensō-ji Temple's massive red lantern at the Kaminarimon Gate weighs approximately 700kg and has become one of Tokyo's most photographed landmarks. The temple's history dates back to 645 CE, making it a spiritual center for Tokyoites for nearly 1,400 years.

Friday, August 8, 2025 – Departure to Kyoto

Day 9

Shinkansen to Kyoto

Bullet train experience to cultural heart of Japan

  • 08:00-09:00: Breakfast at hotel
  • 09:30: Check-out from MIMARU SUITES Tokyo
  • 10:00: Transport to Tokyo Station (10-minute walk from hotel)
  • 10:00-10:45: Tokyo Station exploration
    • Historic station building with beautiful architecture

📚

History & Culture

  • 🏯 Ancient Heritage: Japan has the world's oldest continuous monarchy (Emperor lineage dates back to 660 BCE).
  • 🌸 Contrasts: The country seamlessly blends ancient traditions with cutting-edge technology and pop culture.
  • ⚖️ Harmony: "Wa" (harmony) is central to Japanese culture, emphasizing group needs over individual desires.
  • 🙏 Religion: Most Japanese practice elements of both Shinto (indigenous) and Buddhism, focusing on rituals rather than doctrine.
  • 🎭 Arts: Traditional arts like ikebana (flower arrangement), calligraphy, and the tea ceremony remain important cultural practices.
  • 🎎 Concepts: "Omotenashi" (selfless hospitality) and "wabi-sabi" (finding beauty in imperfection) are key cultural values.
  • 👵 Population: Japan has the world's oldest population and is facing demographic challenges with declining birth rates.

⏳ Quick Timeline

  • • 660 BCE-710 CE: Early periods (mythological founding to Nara)
  • • 710-1185: Classical Japan (Nara & Heian periods)
  • • 1185-1600: Medieval Japan (Kamakura & Muromachi)
  • • 1600-1868: Edo period (Tokugawa shogunate)
  • • 1868-1912: Meiji Restoration (rapid modernization)
  • • 1912-1945: Imperial expansion and World War II
  • • 1945-present: Post-war economic growth, technological leadership

Did You Know?

  • 🍫 Japan has more than 200 flavors of Kit Kat bars, including wasabi, sake, and sweet potato.
  • 🍣 Tokyo has more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city in the world.
  • 🥤 Japan has over 5 million vending machines—one for every 23 people.
  • 🍉 Square watermelons are grown in boxes and sold as luxury items (often costing $100+).
  • 🍜 Slurping noodles is considered polite and shows you're enjoying your meal.
  • 🚅 Punctuality is extremely important—trains average less than 1 minute of delay annually.
  • 🐱 There are more pets than children in Japan due to declining birth rates.
  • 👮 Japan has one of the lowest crime rates in the world.
  • 🏎️ Real-life Mario Kart tours exist in Tokyo (though Nintendo won the lawsuit requiring costume changes).
  • 🏢 The oldest company in the world was in Japan—Kongō Gumi construction company operated for 1,428 years (578-2006).
  • 🛌 Capsule hotels were invented in Japan to provide affordable overnight accommodation.

What to Buy

  • 🥢 Traditional Crafts: Handmade ceramics, chopsticks, folding fans, ukiyo-e prints, and traditional textiles.
  • 📱 Modern Goods: Electronics, cameras, watches, and Japanese kitchen knives.
  • 💄 Beauty Products: Japanese skincare and makeup brands like Shiseido, SK-II, and DHC.
  • 👕 Fashion: Uniqlo clothing, designer items from Comme des Garçons, Issey Miyake, or Yohji Yamamoto.
  • 🎌 Pop Culture: Anime/manga merchandise, character goods from Studio Ghibli, Pokemon, and other franchises.
  • 🍵 Food Items: Green tea, sake, Japanese snacks and candies, premium instant ramen, and specialty Kit Kats.
  • 📝 Stationery: High-quality notebooks, pens, washi tape, and cute/kawaii items.

Best shopping areas: Ginza (luxury), Shibuya/Harajuku (fashion), Akihabara (electronics/anime), Tokyo Station (souvenirs), and department stores like Tokyu Hands or Don Quijote.

💸 Tax Refund

Foreign visitors can receive tax-free shopping on purchases over ¥5,000 (approximately €30) at participating stores. Look for the "Tax-Free" sign and bring your passport when shopping.

Health & Safety

  • 🚰 Tap Water: Safe to drink throughout Japan.
  • 🏥 Healthcare: World-class medical facilities, though English services may be limited outside major cities.
  • 🛡️ Safety: Tokyo is consistently rated one of the world's safest major cities.
  • 🌋 Natural Disasters: Japan is prone to earthquakes, typhoons, and tsunamis, but has excellent preparedness systems.
  • ☎️ Emergency Numbers:
    • Police: 110
    • Ambulance/Fire: 119
    • Japan Visitor Hotline: 050-3816-2787 (24/7, English support)
