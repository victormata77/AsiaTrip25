Welcome to Singapore

During our three-day stay, we'll experience the best of this dynamic destination—from the futuristic Gardens by the Bay and luxury marina area to the rich heritage districts of Chinatown, Little India, and Arab Street. Our base at Village Hotel Sentosa offers both resort relaxation and easy access to Singapore's cultural highlights, creating the perfect balance of leisure and exploration.

Essential Information

Weather in August

  • • Average high: 30-32°C (86-90°F)
  • • Average low: 25-27°C (77-81°F)
  • • Humidity: Very high (80-90%)
  • • Rainfall: Frequent brief showers
  • • Daylight: Sunrise ~7:00 AM, Sunset ~7:15 PM

Singapore's climate is consistently hot and humid year-round. Air conditioning indoors can be quite cold, so bring light layers. Afternoon showers are common but typically brief.

Currency

  • • Singapore Dollar (SGD/S$)
  • • €1 ≈ S$1.45 (April 2025 rate)
  • • Credit cards widely accepted
  • • Contactless payment very common
  • • ATMs readily available throughout the city

Tipping

Not expected or necessary in Singapore. Most restaurants include a 10% service charge and 7% GST in the bill. Taxis do not expect tips.

Getting Around

  • • MRT (Mass Rapid Transit): Efficient and extensive subway system
  • • Singapore Tourist Pass: Unlimited travel on public transport
  • • Hotel Shuttles: Complimentary shuttles to VivoCity/HarbourFront
  • • Sentosa Express: Monorail from VivoCity to Sentosa stations
  • • Grab: Southeast Asia's ride-hailing app (similar to Uber)
  • • Taxis: Clean, metered, and reliable

Our hotel offers complimentary shuttles to VivoCity mall and HarbourFront MRT station, providing easy access to Singapore's excellent public transport network.

Must-Try Foods

  • • Chicken Rice: Singapore's unofficial national dish
  • • Chili Crab: Seafood specialty with sweet-spicy sauce
  • • Laksa: Spicy noodle soup with coconut milk
  • • Satay: Grilled meat skewers with peanut sauce
  • • Kaya Toast: Coconut jam toast with soft-boiled eggs
  • • Ice Kachang: Shaved ice dessert with colorful toppings

Hawker Centers

These affordable food courts offer authentic local cuisine. Notable ones include Maxwell Food Centre, Lau Pa Sat, and Gluttons Bay. Look for stalls with long queues—they're usually the best!

Language & Connectivity

Languages:

  • • English is an official language and widely spoken
  • • Other official languages: Mandarin, Malay, Tamil
  • • "Singlish" is the local English dialect with unique expressions

Internet & Connectivity:

  • • Free public Wi-Fi available (Wireless@SG network)
  • • High-speed Wi-Fi in most hotels, cafes, and malls
  • • Tourist SIM cards available at the airport
  • • Strong 4G/5G coverage throughout the island

Unlike many Asian destinations, language barriers are rare in Singapore as English is the primary language of education and business.

Rules & Etiquette

  • • Strict littering laws with heavy fines
  • • No eating or drinking on public transportation
  • • Chewing gum is prohibited (not sold in Singapore)
  • • Remove shoes when entering homes and some religious sites
  • • Stand on the left side of escalators
  • • Queue for everything - Singaporeans love orderly lines

Important Note

Singapore has very strict drug laws with severe penalties. The country is known for its cleanliness and order, which comes from adherence to various regulations. Follow all posted rules—fines for violations can be substantial.

Singapore is one of the world's smallest countries but has one of the highest GDP per capita. Despite its tiny size, it's a global financial hub and has the world's busiest transshipment port.

History & Multiculturalism

  • Ancient Origins: Originally known as Temasek, the island was a trading post for the Srivijaya Empire as early as the 3rd century.
  • Colonial Era: Sir Stamford Raffles established a British trading post in 1819, transforming the island into a major port city.
  • Japanese Occupation: During WWII, Singapore fell to Japanese forces (1942-1945) in what Winston Churchill called the "worst disaster" in British military history.
  • Independence: After briefly joining Malaysia, Singapore became an independent republic on August 9, 1965, under the leadership of Lee Kuan Yew.
  • Economic Miracle: In just one generation, Singapore transformed from a third-world country to a first-world nation with virtually no natural resources.
  • Cultural Harmony: Singapore embraces its multicultural heritage through policies promoting racial and religious harmony. The "CMIO" model (Chinese, Malay, Indian, Other) forms the basis of many social policies.

Cultural Identity

Singapore's unique identity comes from its blend of Asian cultures with Western influences. This multicultural heritage is reflected in its architecture, festivals, languages, and especially its food, which combines Chinese, Malay, Indian, and Western traditions into unique Singaporean dishes.

Singapore's Food Culture

  • National Passion: Food is considered a national obsession in Singapore, with eating being described as the unofficial national pastime.
  • Hawker Centers: These open-air food courts are the heart of Singapore's food culture, offering affordable, diverse, and authentic local cuisine. Some hawker stalls have even earned Michelin stars.
  • Cultural Fusion: Singaporean cuisine reflects its multicultural heritage, with dishes blending Chinese, Malay, Indian, and Western influences.
  • Famous Dishes:
    • Hainanese Chicken Rice: Poached chicken with fragrant rice
    • Laksa: Spicy coconut milk-based noodle soup
    • Chili Crab: Singapore's iconic seafood dish
    • Char Kway Teow: Stir-fried rice noodles with dark soy sauce
    • Satay: Grilled meat skewers with peanut sauce
  • Food Heritage: In 2020, Singapore's hawker culture was added to UNESCO's list of Intangible Cultural Heritage, recognizing its cultural significance.

Hawker Center Etiquette

When visiting hawker centers, look for tables with tissue packets or personal items—they're "choped" (reserved). After ordering, wait for your food to be ready before paying. It's customary to clear your own tray when finished. Follow the locals' lead and join the longest queues—they usually lead to the best food!

The Garden City Vision

  • Green Vision: In 1967, Singapore launched its "Garden City" campaign, transforming a concrete jungle into one of the world's greenest urban environments.
  • City in a Garden: Singapore has evolved beyond being a "Garden City" to become a "City in a Garden," where greenery is integrated into the urban landscape.
  • Parks Everywhere: Despite its small size, Singapore has over 350 parks and 4 nature reserves, with 80% of residents living within 400m of a green space.
  • Vertical Gardens: With limited land, Singapore pioneered vertical greenery, with many buildings featuring green walls and rooftop gardens.
  • Sustainable Architecture: Buildings like Parkroyal on Pickering and CapitaGreen incorporate thousands of plants into their designs, creating "hotel-in-a-garden" experiences.
  • Gardens by the Bay: This 101-hectare nature park features Supertrees (vertical gardens up to 50m tall), two conservatories (Flower Dome and Cloud Forest), and serves as Singapore's horticultural showcase.

Singapore's transformation into a green city wasn't just aesthetic—it was a deliberate strategy to differentiate itself from other Asian cities, provide a high quality of life, and create a sustainable urban environment despite limited natural resources.

Did You Know?

  • Singapore is one of only three city-states in the world, alongside Monaco and Vatican City.
  • The Singapore Sling cocktail was created at the Long Bar in Raffles Hotel around 1915.
  • Singapore's Changi Airport has been voted the world's best airport for 12 consecutive years (2013-2025).
  • The national symbol of Singapore is the Merlion—a mythical creature with a lion's head and fish's body.
  • Singapore has one of the world's lowest crime rates and is consistently ranked among the safest countries.
  • Despite its tiny size, Singapore is the world's fourth-leading financial center after New York, London, and Hong Kong.
  • Singapore has the world's highest percentage of millionaires, with nearly 10% of households having at least $1 million in disposable wealth.
  • The Singapore Botanic Gardens is the only tropical garden to be honored as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Singapore's tap water is among the cleanest in the world and is perfectly safe to drink directly from the tap.
  • Singapore's Night Safari was the world's first nocturnal wildlife park when it opened in 1994.

Sentosa Island: Your Resort Home

  • Island Transformation: Once known as Pulau Blakang Mati ("Island of Death from Behind" in Malay), Sentosa was renamed in 1972 to a Sanskrit word meaning "peace and tranquility."
  • Man-Made Beaches: Sentosa's beaches (Siloso, Palawan, and Tanjong) feature imported sand from Indonesia and Malaysia to create idyllic shorelines.
  • Top Attractions:
    • Universal Studios Singapore: Southeast Asia's first and only Universal Studios theme park
    • S.E.A. Aquarium: Home to more than 100,000 marine animals
    • Adventure Cove Waterpark: Water slides and marine encounters
    • Mega Adventure Park: 450m zipline across the jungle canopy
    • Madame Tussauds Singapore: Wax museum featuring local and international celebrities
  • Unique Transport: You can reach Sentosa via cable car, monorail (Sentosa Express), walking across the Sentosa Boardwalk, or by car.
  • Luxury Hub: Sentosa is home to some of Singapore's most exclusive residences, with beachfront properties commanding premium prices.
  • Historical Significance: Fort Siloso on Sentosa is Singapore's only preserved coastal fort and played a significant role during WWII.
