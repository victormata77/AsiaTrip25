Essential Information

Weather in August

  • • Average high: 30°C (86°F)
  • • Average low: 25°C (77°F)
  • • Humidity: High (75-85%)
  • • Rainfall: Moderate, brief showers possible
  • • Daylight: Sunrise ~5:50 AM, Sunset ~6:30 PM
  • • Ocean temperature: 28-29°C (82-84°F)

August is generally pleasant in the Maldives with plenty of sunshine despite being technically part of the monsoon season. Occasional brief showers provide a refreshing break from the tropical sun. The water is warm and perfect for swimming year-round.

Getting There & Around

  • • Malé to Resort: 30-45 minute seaplane transfer
  • • Seaplane Hours: Operates only during daylight (6:00 AM - 4:00 PM)
  • • Resort Transportation: Golf carts for longer distances
  • • Walking: Wooden boardwalks connect all overwater villas
  • • Island Size: Small enough to walk around in 15-20 minutes

The seaplane journey to the resort is an experience in itself, offering breathtaking aerial views of the atolls. We've secured a 50% discount on the seaplane transfer as part of our accommodation package. Note that seaplanes have strict luggage weight restrictions (typically 20kg per person + 5kg hand luggage).

Resort Facilities

  • • Overwater Villa: Private deck, direct ocean access, indoor/outdoor shower
  • • Dining: Multiple restaurants including underwater dining experience
  • • Spa: Overwater treatment pavilions with glass floors
  • • Pools: Main infinity pool with ocean views
  • • Fitness Center: Air-conditioned with ocean views
  • • Kids Club: Supervised activities for younger children
  • • Water Sports Center: Equipment rental and excursion booking

Internet Access

Complimentary high-speed Wi-Fi is available throughout the resort, including in your villa. Coverage might occasionally be affected by weather conditions.

Water Activities

  • • Snorkeling: House reef accessible directly from villas
  • • Diving: PADI-certified dive center with courses for beginners
  • • Non-motorized Water Sports: Kayaking, paddleboarding, windsurfing
  • • Motorized Water Sports: Jet skiing, parasailing, wakeboarding
  • • Excursions: Dolphin watching, fishing trips, island hopping
  • • Marine Wildlife: Manta rays, reef sharks, turtles, colorful fish

Snorkeling Tip

The best snorkeling times are during high tide when water currents are minimal. The resort provides complimentary snorkeling equipment for all guests. Consider bringing your own mask if you wear prescription glasses.

Dining Experiences

  • • Alifaan: All-day dining restaurant with international cuisine
  • • Crusoe's: Beach grill serving fresh seafood and steaks
  • • The Lab: Underwater restaurant for special dining experiences
  • • Mahurab: Overwater bar and lounge for sunset cocktails
  • • Eats & Beats: Pool bar with light snacks and refreshments
  • • In-Villa Dining: 24-hour room service
  • • Special Arrangements: Private beach dinners, floating breakfast

Dining Recommendation

The underwater restaurant requires advance reservation (ideally before arrival). For a memorable experience, consider booking a private beach dinner under the stars on one evening of our stay.

Essential Travel Tips

  • • Currency: USD widely accepted at resorts, credit cards (Visa/Mastercard) preferred
  • • Time Zone: Maldives Time (MVT) is UTC+5, 3 hours behind Singapore
  • • Language: Dhivehi is official, but English is widely spoken at the resort
  • • Electricity: 220-240V, UK-style three-pin plugs (adapters available at resort)
  • • Dress Code: Resort casual, modest attire for dining areas
  • • Tipping: Not required but appreciated (10-15% customary for good service)
  • • Health: No vaccines required, but travel insurance recommended

Important Note

The Maldives is a Muslim country with strict laws on alcohol and modest dress. However, these restrictions don't apply to resort islands like ours. Respect local customs if visiting inhabited islands.

📉 Low-Lying Nation

The Maldives is the world's lowest country with an average elevation of just 1.5 meters (4.9 feet) above sea level. The highest natural point in the country is just 2.4 meters (7.9 feet) above sea level, making the Maldives particularly vulnerable to rising sea levels due to climate change.

🐠

Incredible Marine Life

  • 🪸 Coral Reefs: The Maldives is home to approximately 5% of the world's coral reefs, supporting over 2,000 species of fish and other marine life.
  • 🦈 Manta Rays: The Maldives has the world's largest population of reef manta rays, which can be seen year-round at various cleaning stations.
  • 🐋 Whale Sharks: The gentle giants of the ocean can be spotted throughout the year, particularly in the South Ari Atoll.
  • 🐢 Sea Turtles: Five of the world's seven species of sea turtles can be found in Maldivian waters, including green turtles and hawksbill turtles.
  • 🦈 Reef Sharks: Several species of non-aggressive reef sharks are common, including blacktip reef sharks, whitetip reef sharks, and nurse sharks.
  • 🐬 Dolphins: Spinner dolphins and bottlenose dolphins are frequently spotted playing in the channels between islands.

🤿 Snorkeling at Our Resort

The house reef at Radisson Blu Resort is known for its diverse marine life, including colorful reef fish, rays, reef sharks, and occasionally turtles. The waters surrounding the resort feature visibility up to 30 meters, creating perfect conditions for underwater exploration.

🏮

Maldivian Culture & Traditions

  • 🧠 Dhivehi Culture: The Maldivian culture, known as Dhivehi, is a unique blend of influences from South Asia, Arabia, and East Africa.
  • 🥁 Bodu Beru: Traditional Maldivian music featuring drums made of coconut wood, often performed at resorts for guests.
  • 👗 Libaas: Traditional Maldivian costume consisting of a long dress for women and a sarong paired with a shirt for men.
  • 🧶 Crafts: Traditional crafts include mat weaving (Thundu Kunaa), coir rope making, lacquer work, and traditional boat (dhoni) building.
  • 🍲 Cuisine: Fresh seafood, coconut, and spices form the basis of Maldivian cuisine, with popular dishes including mas huni (tuna and coconut), garudhiya (fish soup), and hedhikaa (savory snacks).
  • 🤝 Hospitality: Maldivians are known for their warm hospitality, reflected in the resort industry's high service standards.

🎭 Cultural Performances

Our resort occasionally offers cultural evenings featuring Bodu Beru performances, where drums start slowly and gradually build to a faster rhythm. Guests are often invited to join in the dancing, making it an interactive cultural experience.

💡

Did You Know?

  • 🗺️ The Maldives is the smallest Asian country both by land area and population.
  • 📝 The word "atoll" is derived from the Dhivehi word "atholhu."
  • 🌊 The Maldives was the first country to hold an underwater cabinet meeting in 2009 to highlight the threat of climate change.
  • 🥥 Coconut is considered the most important plant in the Maldives and is used in many aspects of daily life, from food and drink to construction materials and handicrafts.
  • 🍽️ The Maldives is home to the world's first underwater restaurant, opened in 2005 at the Conrad Maldives Rangali Island resort.
  • 📏 The unique geography of the Maldives means that no island is more than 1.8 meters (5.9 feet) above sea level.
  • 🏨 The Maldives was the first country in South Asia to introduce private island resorts in the 1970s, pioneering the luxury tourism model that it's famous for today.
  • 🏝️ Every resort in the Maldives occupies its own private island, leading to the concept of "one island, one resort."
  • 📆 The Maldives follows both the Islamic calendar and the Gregorian calendar.
  • 🐚 The Maldivian rufiyaa, the national currency, gets its name from the cowry shells once used as currency.
♻️

Sustainability in Paradise

  • 🌡️ Climate Change: As the world's lowest-lying nation, the Maldives is particularly vulnerable to rising sea levels and has become a vocal advocate for climate action globally.
  • 🪸 Coral Regeneration: Many resorts, including ours, participate in coral regeneration programs to help restore damaged reefs.
  • 🚯 Plastic Reduction: The Maldives has implemented various initiatives to reduce single-use plastics, with many resorts eliminating plastic straws, bottles, and toiletry containers.
  • ☀️ Renewable Energy: Solar power is increasingly being adopted across resorts to reduce dependency on diesel generators.
  • 💧 Desalination: With no natural freshwater sources, the Maldives relies on desalination technology to produce drinking water.
  • 🐠 Marine Conservation: Protected marine areas and fishing regulations help preserve the rich biodiversity of Maldivian waters.

🌿 How You Can Help

During our stay, we can contribute to sustainability efforts by using reef-safe sunscreen, avoiding touching or stepping on coral while snorkeling, conserving water, and participating in any conservation activities offered by the resort, such as coral planting or beach clean-ups.
