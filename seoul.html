Welcome to Seoul

Seoul is a captivating blend of ancient traditions and cutting-edge innovation. As South Korea's capital, this dynamic metropolis offers an unforgettable mix of historic palaces, bustling markets, tranquil temples, and futuristic architecture. During our four-night stay, we'll experience both the rich cultural heritage and the vibrant contemporary energy that makes Seoul one of Asia's most exciting destinations.

Essential Information

Weather in August

  • • Average high: 30-32°C (86-90°F)
  • • Average low: 22-25°C (71-77°F)
  • • Humidity: High (70-80%)
  • • Rainfall: Monsoon season, frequent showers
  • • Daylight: Sunrise ~5:30 AM, Sunset ~7:30 PM

Pack light, breathable clothing, rain gear, and a light jacket for air-conditioned spaces.

Currency

  • • Korean Won (₩/KRW)
  • • €1 ≈ ₩1,450 (April 2025 rate)
  • • Credit cards widely accepted
  • • ATMs available throughout the city
  • • Foreign exchange at airport, hotels, banks

Tipping

Not expected in South Korea. Service charge often included at upscale restaurants.

Getting Around

  • • T-Money Card: Essential for subway, bus, and some taxis
  • • Subway: Clean, efficient network connecting most attractions
  • • Buses: Extensive network with English displays
  • • Taxis: Affordable and plentiful
  • • Kakao T app: Convenient for booking taxis

Our hotel is near Samseong Station on Line 2 (green line), offering easy access to many popular destinations.

Must-Try Foods

  • • Bibimbap: Mixed rice bowl with vegetables, meat, and egg
  • • Bulgogi: Marinated grilled beef
  • • Korean BBQ: Grilled meat at your table
  • • Kimchi: Fermented cabbage (served with most meals)
  • • Tteokbokki: Spicy rice cakes
  • • Bingsu: Shaved ice dessert with toppings

Dining Etiquette

Wait for elders to start eating, use both hands when receiving food/drinks, don't stick chopsticks upright in rice.

Language & Communication

Useful Korean Phrases:

Hello 안녕하세요 (Annyeong-haseyo)
Thank you 감사합니다 (Kamsahamnida)
Yes/No 네/아니요 (Ne/Aniyo)
Excuse me 실례합니다 (Sillyehamnida)
Where is... 어디입니까? (Eodiimnikka?)
How much? 얼마예요? (Eolmayeyo?)

Many signs are in English, especially in tourist areas. Download Papago app for real-time translation.

Cultural Tips

  • • Bow slightly when greeting people (especially elders)
  • • Remove shoes when entering homes or certain restaurants
  • • Accept items (money, business cards) with both hands
  • • Public displays of affection are uncommon
  • • Point with your entire hand rather than index finger
  • • Cover your mouth when using a toothpick

Important Note

Respect is highly valued in Korean culture, particularly toward elders and people in positions of authority.

📚

History & Culture

  • 🏯 Ancient Heritage: Seoul has been Korea's capital for over 600 years, since the Joseon Dynasty established it in 1392.
  • 🎭 K-Wave: South Korea's global cultural influence (Hallyu) includes K-pop, K-dramas, beauty products, and cuisine.
  • 🎂 Age Culture: Koreans calculate age differently—everyone is 1 at birth and gains a year every New Year's Day.
  • 📱 Technology: Seoul has the world's fastest internet speeds and highest smartphone ownership rates.
  • 📚 Education Focus: Education is highly valued, with students often studying until late evening at after-school academies.
  • 👪 Family Structure: Confucian values emphasize respect for elders and strong family bonds.
  • 🚧 Division: Korea was divided after World War II, creating North and South Korea with the DMZ separating them since 1953.

⏳ Quick Timeline

  • 1392-1910: Joseon Dynasty
  • 1910-1945: Japanese occupation
  • 1945: Liberation and division
  • 1950-1953: Korean War
  • 1960s-1980s: Rapid industrialization
  • 1988: Seoul Olympics
  • 1997: Asian Financial Crisis
  • 2000s-present: Global cultural and technological influence
💡

Did You Know?

  • 💳 South Korea has the world's highest credit card usage rate per capita.
  • 🚇 Seoul's subway has free WiFi and heated seats in winter.
  • 🕺 "Gangnam Style" is named after our hotel's neighborhood—Gangnam means "south of the river."
  • ✈️ Seoul's Incheon Airport has consistently ranked as the world's best airport.
  • 4️⃣ Many buildings in Korea don't have a 4th floor, as the number 4 sounds like the word for "death."
  • 🛍️ Seoul has one of the world's largest underground shopping malls (COEX).
  • 👶 South Korea has the world's lowest birth rate (less than 1 child per woman).
  • 🚧 The border between North and South Korea (DMZ) is the most heavily militarized border in the world.
  • 💰 Despite its small size, South Korea is the 10th largest economy in the world.
  • 💨 Fan death is a common superstition—many Koreans believe sleeping with a fan on in a closed room can be fatal.
  • 🔒 South Korea is one of the safest countries in the world with extremely low crime rates.
🛍️

What to Buy

  • 💄 K-Beauty Products: Face masks, serums, and cosmetics from brands like Innisfree, Nature Republic, and COSRX.
  • 🎨 Traditional Crafts: Hanji (handmade paper), celadon pottery, and hanbok-inspired accessories.
  • 🎤 K-Pop Merchandise: Official albums, posters, and merchandise from K-pop groups.
  • 📱 Technology: Electronics and gadgets, often available before worldwide release.
  • 🍭 Korean Snacks: Unique flavors of familiar snacks (honey butter chips, banana milk).
  • 🍵 Tea and Ginseng: Korean red ginseng products and traditional teas.
  • 🍶 Soju and Makgeolli: Traditional Korean alcoholic beverages with unique flavors.

Best shopping areas: Myeongdong, Dongdaemun Market, COEX Mall, and Insadong for traditional items.

💸 Tax Refund

Tourists can get a tax refund on purchases over ₩30,000 (approximately €20) at participating stores. Ask for a tax-free receipt and claim at the airport before departure.

🛡️

Health & Safety

  • 🚰 Tap Water: Safe to drink, though many Koreans prefer filtered or bottled water.
  • 🏥 Healthcare: Excellent medical facilities with English-speaking staff at major hospitals.
  • 🛡️ Safety: Seoul consistently ranks among the world's safest cities with low crime rates.
  • 🌫️ Air Quality: Can be poor during certain times of year, especially spring dust season.
  • ☎️ Emergency Numbers:
    • Police: 112
    • Ambulance/Fire: 119
    • Tourist Hotline: 1330 (24/7, English support)

🏥 Travel Insurance

Our family policy (#TRV-2025-61792) covers medical emergencies and evacuation. Keep digital and physical copies of insurance details.

Recommended Hospitals with English Services:

  • Seoul National University Hospital (near Hyehwa Station)
  • Severance Hospital (near Sinchon Station)
  • Samsung Medical Center (near Irwon Station, closer to our hotel)
📱

Tech & Connectivity

  • ⚡ Internet Speed: South Korea has the world's fastest internet (avg. 215 Mbps).
  • 📶 Wi-Fi Access: Free Wi-Fi available in most public places, including subway stations.
  • 📱 Mobile Data: Consider purchasing a local SIM card or pocket Wi-Fi device.
  • 5️⃣ 5G Coverage: Nationwide 5G network with excellent coverage in Seoul.
  • 📲 Essential Apps:
    • Kakao Map: Better than Google Maps in Korea
    • Kakao T: For taxi booking
    • Naver Map: Alternative map with excellent public transit info
    • Papago: Translation app specialized for Korean
    • Korea Subway: For navigating Seoul's extensive metro system

📱 SIM Card Options

KT, SK Telecom, and LG U+ offer tourist SIM cards at the airport. 5-day unlimited data plans cost approximately ₩30,000-40,000 (€20-27).
