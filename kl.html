Welcome to Kuala Lumpur

Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia's vibrant capital, offers a fascinating blend of modern urban landscapes and rich cultural heritage. This dynamic city showcases gleaming skyscrapers like the iconic Petronas Twin Towers alongside colorful temples and bustling markets. During our two-night stay, we'll experience the city's diverse attractions, from sacred caves to luxurious shopping malls, enjoying Malaysia's famous multicultural cuisine and warm hospitality.

Essential Information

Weather in August

  • • Average high: 32-34°C (90-93°F)
  • • Average low: 23-25°C (73-77°F)
  • • Humidity: Very high (80-90%)
  • • Rainfall: Frequent afternoon showers
  • • Daylight: Sunrise ~7:00 AM, Sunset ~7:30 PM

Pack lightweight, breathable clothing, a compact umbrella or rain jacket, and stay hydrated in the tropical heat.

Currency

  • • Malaysian Ringgit (RM/MYR)
  • • €1 ≈ RM5.10 (April 2025 rate)
  • • Cash widely used but credit cards accepted in hotels, malls
  • • ATMs readily available throughout the city
  • • Money changers offer good rates at malls and central areas

Tipping

Not generally expected in Malaysia. Most high-end restaurants include a 10% service charge. Taxis don't expect tips but rounding up is appreciated.

Getting Around

  • • Grab App: Malaysia's popular ride-sharing service
  • • LRT/MRT: Clean, efficient rail network
  • • Monorail: Connects major tourist spots
  • • Taxis: Use metered taxis or pre-arrange through hotel
  • • Walking: Possible in some areas but heat can be challenging

Our hotel in Bukit Bintang provides excellent access to shopping and dining. The concierge can arrange transportation for our excursions including premium hotel cars.

Must-Try Foods

  • • Nasi Lemak: Coconut rice with sambal, fried anchovies, peanuts
  • • Satay: Grilled meat skewers with peanut sauce
  • • Roti Canai: Flaky flatbread with curry dip
  • • Char Kway Teow: Stir-fried flat rice noodles
  • • Laksa: Spicy noodle soup with coconut milk
  • • Durian: The infamous "king of fruits" (very pungent!)

Dining Etiquette

Wash hands before eating; use right hand for eating if using hands; asking for a fork and spoon is perfectly acceptable; halal food is widely available.

Language & Communication

Useful Malay Phrases:

Hello Selamat (se-la-mat)
Thank you Terima kasih (te-ri-ma ka-seh)
Yes/No Ya/Tidak (ya/ti-dak)
Excuse me Maaf (ma-af)
How much? Berapa harga? (be-ra-pa har-ga)
Delicious Sedap (se-dap)

English is widely spoken in Kuala Lumpur, especially in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. Most signs are in both Malay and English.

Cultural Tips

  • • Dress modestly at religious sites (covered shoulders/knees)
  • • Remove shoes when entering mosques, temples, and homes
  • • Use right hand for eating, giving, and receiving
  • • Pointing with index finger is impolite; use thumb instead
  • • Respect Muslim prayer times (particularly on Fridays)
  • • Ask permission before photographing locals

Important Note

Malaysia is a multicultural society with Malay, Chinese, and Indian influences. Religious tolerance is valued, and the country blends Islamic traditions with modern attitudes.

Malaysia is divided into two regions: Peninsular Malaysia and East Malaysia (on Borneo island), separated by the South China Sea. The country is known for its cultural diversity, with significant Malay, Chinese, and Indian populations contributing to its rich heritage.

History & Culture

  • Melting Pot: Malaysia's unique identity emerges from centuries of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and European influences.
  • Colonial Past: Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonization shaped the country before independence in 1957.
  • Federation: Malaysia was formed in 1963 when Malaya united with Singapore (which later separated), Sabah, and Sarawak.
  • Multicultural Society: While Islam is the official religion, freedom of worship is protected with significant Buddhist, Hindu, and Christian populations.
  • Arts: Traditional crafts include batik (fabric art), pewter crafting, and kite-making (wau).
  • Cuisine: Malaysian food reflects its cultural diversity, combining Malay, Chinese, and Indian culinary traditions.
  • Architecture: Features a blend of Islamic, Chinese, colonial, and ultramodern influences.

Cultural Diversity

  • • Malay: Indigenous culture, traditional music (gamelan), Islamic influences
  • • Chinese: Significant community since 15th century, maintained cultural traditions
  • • Indian: Primarily Tamil heritage, Hindu festivals, distinctive cuisine
  • • Indigenous: Orang Asli and other native communities with unique traditions
  • • Peranakan: Unique blend of Chinese and Malay cultures with distinctive food and architecture

Did You Know?

  • Malaysia is home to the world's oldest rainforest, Taman Negara, estimated to be over 130 million years old.
  • The Petronas Twin Towers were the world's tallest buildings from 1998 to 2004 (452 meters).
  • Malaysia is one of the 17 megadiverse countries, harboring an estimated 20% of the world's animal species.
  • The Rafflesia, the world's largest flower (up to 1 meter in diameter), is native to Malaysian rainforests.
  • Malaysia is the second-largest producer of palm oil globally, after Indonesia.
  • Kuala Lumpur means "muddy confluence" in Malay, referring to the meeting of the Gombak and Klang rivers.
  • Malaysia's monarchy is unique—nine hereditary state rulers take turns serving as king (Yang di-Pertuan Agong) for five-year terms.
  • The Malaysian flag is called "Jalur Gemilang" (Stripes of Glory).
  • Penang's Kek Lok Si is the largest Buddhist temple in Southeast Asia.
  • Mount Kinabalu in Malaysian Borneo is the highest peak in the country at 4,095 meters.

What to Buy

  • Batik Fabric: Colorful handcrafted textiles with beautiful patterns and designs.
  • Pewter Products: Malaysia (particularly Royal Selangor) is famous for high-quality pewter craftsmanship.
  • Handicrafts: Woven items, wood carvings, and songket (gold-threaded fabric).
  • Spices & Tea: Exotic spices, specialty teas, and local coffee varieties.
  • Tropical Fruits: Packaged durian products, dried fruits, and fruit preserves.
  • Fashion: Modern Malaysian designer clothing that incorporates traditional elements.
  • Chocolate: Malaysia produces excellent cocoa, with unique local flavors like durian chocolate.

Best shopping areas: Bukit Bintang district (near our hotel), Central Market for handicrafts, Suria KLCC mall at Petronas Towers, and Pavilion KL shopping mall.

Shopping Tips

Feel free to bargain at markets like Central Market and Petaling Street (politely), but fixed prices are the norm in malls and department stores. Look for the Malaysian handicraft logo for authentic local crafts.

Health & Safety

  • Tap Water: Not recommended for drinking; stick to bottled water.
  • Healthcare: Excellent medical facilities in Kuala Lumpur, especially private hospitals.
  • Safety: Kuala Lumpur is generally safe for tourists; take normal precautions against pickpocketing.
  • Weather: Be prepared for sudden tropical downpours and high humidity.
  • Emergency Numbers:
    • Police: 999
    • Ambulance/Fire: 999
    • Tourist Police: 03-2149-6590
