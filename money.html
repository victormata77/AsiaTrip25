Money Overview

Managing money effectively across five different countries with different currencies will be a key aspect of our journey. This guide outlines currency information, payment methods, budget planning, and tipping customs for South Korea, Japan, Singapore, Malaysia, and the Maldives.

Our financial strategy focuses on a mix of payment methods to ensure we always have access to funds and can pay for goods and services in the most convenient and cost-effective way. Key components include:

  • Using multi-currency cards (Revolut) to minimize exchange fees
  • Our Caixabank Visa card as primary payment method with no foreign transaction fees
  • American Express Platinum as backup with travel benefits and purchase protection
  • Carrying some local cash for each country
  • Understanding local payment systems and mobile payment options
  • Following local tipping customs
  • Tracking expenses through a digital tool

ATM & Money Exchange Tips

ATM Best Practices:

  • Use bank ATMs in well-lit, secure locations (avoid third-party ATMs)
  • Withdraw larger amounts less frequently to minimize fees
  • Always choose to be charged in local currency (reject dynamic currency conversion)
  • Shield PIN entry and be aware of surroundings
  • Keep ATM receipts until reconciling transactions

Money Exchange Tips:

  • Avoid airport currency exchanges (except in emergencies)
  • Research current exchange rates before transactions
  • Count money carefully before leaving counter
  • Keep exchange receipts for possible return exchanges
  • In Singapore & Malaysia, licensed money changers often offer better rates than banks

Recommended ATM Networks by Country:

  • South Korea: Look for Global ATM Service signs, Hana Bank, KB Kookmin Bank
  • Japan: 7-Eleven ATMs, Japan Post Bank ATMs, AEON Bank ATMs
  • Singapore: DBS, OCBC, UOB Bank ATMs all widely available
  • Malaysia: CIMB, Maybank, Public Bank ATMs
  • Maldives: Resort has ATM service (with fees), better to bring USD

Tipping Customs

Tipping customs vary significantly across Asian countries. Understanding local expectations will help us navigate social situations appropriately and budget correctly. Here's a country-by-country guide:

South Korea

No Tipping Culture

Restaurants

No tipping expected. Service charge may be included at high-end restaurants.

Taxis

No tipping. Round up the fare if convenient, but not required.

Hotels

No tipping for bellhops or housekeeping. Service charge often included.

Tour Guides

Optional, but appreciated for exceptional service. ‚Ç©10,000-20,000 for a day tour.

Important Note: Attempting to tip in some situations might be considered offensive or confusing. Service staff are paid a proper wage.

Japan

Tipping is Not Practice

Restaurants

Tipping can be seen as rude. Bills are paid at the counter in many places.

Taxis

No tipping. Drivers may refuse tips or return money.

Hotels

No tipping. Service charge is included in luxury hotels.

Tour Guides

For private guides, a small gift from your home country is more appropriate than cash.

Cultural Note: In Japan, good service is expected as standard and not dependent on tips. Offering a tip may cause confusion or even be seen as insulting.

Singapore

Service Charge Common

Restaurants

10% service charge + 7% GST usually included. No additional tip needed.

Taxis

Rounding up fare is common, but not expected.

Hotels

Service charge included. S$2-5 for porters is optional.

Tour Guides

S$10-20 per day for good service is appreciated but not required.

Look for: "Service charge and GST included" on bills. If not stated, a 10% tip may be appropriate in high-end establishments.

Malaysia

Service Charge Common

Restaurants

10% service charge + 6% sales tax usually included. Check bill.

Taxis

Rounding up fare is common. No percentage-based tipping.

Hotels

RM5-10 for porters is customary. Service charge included otherwise.

Tour Guides

RM20-50 per day depending on group size and service quality.

Note: At smaller establishments without service charge, a small tip (5-10%) for good service is appreciated but not mandatory.

Maldives

Tipping Expected at Resorts

Restaurants

10% service charge is included, but additional 5-10% in USD for exceptional service is appreciated.

Resort Staff

$3-5 per day for housekeeping, left daily or at end of stay.

Boat/Excursion Staff

$5-10 per person for half-day excursions.

Personal Butler/Villa Host

$10-20 per day, typically given at end of stay.

Resort Tipping: Radisson Blu Resort has a staff tipping box at reception. You can also give individual tips to staff who provide excellent service.

Our Family Tipping Strategy

Small Bills

Keep a supply of small denominations in local currency (and USD for Maldives) for occasional tipping.

Small Gifts

Bring some small items from Spain (chocolates, pens, etc.) as alternative tips for guides in Japan/Korea.

Service Charge Awareness

Always check bills to see if service charge is included before deciding whether to tip.

Tipping Budget
